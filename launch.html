The Missing Piece

Thinking about a feeling
is not the same as feeling it.

You've done therapy. You've journaled. You've read the books. You can name every pattern you have. And the feeling is still stuck.

That's because the feeling lives in your body — the tight chest, the clenched jaw, the pit in your stomach. All the thinking in the world won't move it. It needs to be felt. Actually felt. For about 90 seconds. Then it releases, you can see what's yours and what isn't, and you make a different choice.

That's what Unsnag does. Six steps. Under ten minutes. No appointment, no practitioner, no homework. It doesn't make you feel like a patient or like something's wrong with you. It's just a thing you reach for — and every time you do, you're building a new default. That's how things actually change. Not by understanding the pattern better. By feeling the feeling underneath it enough times that you stop reacting from that old place.

How It Works

Six steps. The feeling moves.
You make a different choice.

Open it when something fires — someone's tone, an unanswered text, the panic before a hard conversation. Six steps, under ten minutes. Then you close it and go live your life. Do it enough times and the old reactive pattern starts to lose its grip.

U
Unload

Dump it out.

What just happened? What are you spiraling about? Type or talk — no filter. Get the overthinking out of your head so you can drop into your body.

N
Name

Tap what you're feeling.

Anxious? Guilty? Resentful? Overwhelmed? Tap the ones that fit. Just naming it turns the volume down — it shifts the activation out of your amygdala and into the part of your brain that can actually do something with it.

S
Sense

Find it in your body.

Clenched jaw? Tight chest? Pit in your stomach? This is where you stop thinking about the feeling and start actually locating it.

N
Notice

Just feel it. Don't fix it.

A feeling fully felt lasts about 90 seconds — that's how long the neurochemical wave takes to peak and pass. The timer holds space for that. You don't have to do anything. Just be with it. Most people feel the grip loosen before the timer ends.

A
Ask

What does it actually want to say?

Let it out raw — the thing you'd never say out loud. Not the spiral. What's underneath it.

G
Go

Make a different choice.

Before this, you would've spiraled. Apologized. Gone quiet. Taken it on. Now you've actually felt the feeling and seen what's yours — so what's available to you now that wasn't before? Say the thing. Let them be disappointed. Not your circus. Every time you make that choice, you're building a new default.

✦ Yes, there's a little AI in here

It shows you what's yours — and what was never yours to carry.

After you move through the steps, the AI reads what you wrote and lays it out clearly: your frustration, your unspoken need, the choice you've been avoiding — that's yours to own. Their reaction, their mood, whether they get upset — never yours. For someone who's been carrying everyone else's stuff their whole life, seeing this separated is usually where something clicks.

Frequently asked questions

Is this therapy? +

No. It's a self-guided processing tool — not a clinical intervention and not a replacement for therapy. Think of it as what you reach for between sessions. At 11pm when the spiral hits and your therapist isn't available. In the car after a conversation that activated something old. Therapy gives you insight. Unsnag gives the feeling somewhere to go.

Is this just journaling? +

No. Writing is involved in the Unload step, but the goal isn't to think about the feeling — it's to get it out of your head so you can move into your body. The 6-step process is somatic, not reflective. Journaling gives you more thoughts. Unsnag gives the feeling somewhere to go.

I don't have time for another wellness thing. +

A full session is under 10 minutes. The 90-second Notice step is the only timed part. This isn't a wellness practice you dedicate yourself to. It's a thing you reach for when something fires — in the moment, not as homework.

Do I need to know anything about somatic work? +

Not at all. The app guides you through every step. Just show up with whatever's going on.

Will this actually change anything, or just help in the moment? +

Both — in that order. Each session processes what's happening right now. Over time, you're building a new default response. The pattern starts to lose its grip. You'll notice it in small moments first: you don't spiral over the text. You say the thing. You let someone be disappointed without it destroying you.
