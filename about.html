Who Unsnag Is For

You're warm, funny, generous —
and somehow still convinced
you're the problem.

You're not. You've just never had a way to actually process what you're feeling in the moment — instead of carrying it for hours. Unsnag is a guided emotional processing app, built for you. By someone who needed it too.

Does This Sound Like You?

Hi. I see you.

01
You'd rather disappear than disappoint someone.

Not because you're weak — because somewhere along the way you figured out that keeping the peace was safer than being honest about what you needed. That made sense then. It's exhausting now.

02
When someone seems off with you, you assume it's your fault.

Doesn't matter if you did anything. Doesn't matter if you literally can't think of a single reason they'd be upset. The spiral starts anyway. You're already apologizing in your head.

Why I Built This

I was all of the above.
Still am sometimes.
This is what finally worked.

— Afi Catena, founder of Unsnag

I held everything together for everyone. I was terrified of disappointing anyone. I could explain my patterns perfectly — had been in therapy, had done the reading, genuinely understood where it all came from. And I still fell apart over an unanswered text. Still felt guilty for things that had nothing to do with me. Still couldn't speak up without spending three days recovering from it.

What changed wasn't more understanding. It was learning how to actually feel what was happening in my body — not think about it, not manage it — and let it move through. That sounds like a lot. It isn't. Once I had a simple process for it, things shifted in ways that years of insight never touched.

I'm a mom. A regular person. I built Unsnag because the tool I needed didn't exist — and because the gap between knowing your patterns and actually being able to change them is real, it's solvable, and it doesn't require another commitment. Just a few minutes when you need it. Then you close it and go live your life.

Here's what I actually believe: you have something real and irreplaceable to offer. And you can't fully give it when you're spending your energy managing everyone else's reactions. When you stop needing their approval to feel okay — not by deciding to, but because something in your body actually shifted — you get yourself back. That's the whole point. Not to fix you. To free you.
